Experience

May - August, 2019

Google Summer of Code

Apertus° Open Source Cinema

The AXIOM Beta, a cinema-grade camera designed by Apertus° Association, currently features PCIe x1 connectors on the mainboard, which are capable of transferring data at a rate of 6Gbps+. Several high-speed plugin modules like HDMI, DP, or SDI can be plugged into these, thereby providing additional I/O functionalities. The AXIOM Beta lacked a USB 3.0 interface for direct transmission of RAW video data to a connected PC for recording/streaming purposes.

This project was about implementing a gearwork on the Lattice FPGA that can stream live 4K raw 12-bit video at 20+ FPS through a USB 3.0 port using the LVDS connection exposed by the PCIe connectors on the camera. I am currently optimizing the gearwork for the final market launch, and in the current state, it is providing a throughput of 3.0 Gbps with a bit error rate of less than 10-14 through the USB 3.0 port.
